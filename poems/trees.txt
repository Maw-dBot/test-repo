Roots reach deep into the dark, cool earth,
While branches stretch toward the open sky.
Each ring of wood records another year,
A quiet chronicle of sun and rain.

In spring they dress in tender, new-grown green;
In autumn, blaze with amber, gold, and red.
Through winter storms they hold their patient ground,
And wait for warmth to wake the sleeping buds.

They shelter birds and shade the summer grass,
Convert our breath to something we can breathe.
What better neighbors could we ever ask
Than those who only ask for light and rain?
